Properties of Soapmaking Oils

Visualizing the Fatty Acid Compositions of Natural Oils used in Cold-Process Soapmaking

Every oil has its own properties as an acidic triglyceride, determined by the weights of the fatty acid molecules they are made up of. Adjusting the proportion of an oil in a triglyceride mix eventually changes the characteristics of the soap bar as an end result.

For example, coconut oil increases the hardness of a bar and the fluffiness of the lather quality, owing to its high percentage of lauric acid (around 50%)[1]. Jojoba Oil on the other hand, contains only around 10% oleic acid[2] and will therefore contribute towards the soap's conditioning properties.

Each triglyceride contributes to more than 1 property of the soap bar, notwithstanding the final aroma, colour, acceptance to other fragrance additives to name a few. We can now see that a data visualization of the different triglyceride properties of each individual oil would be of good use to any soapmaking enthusiast on the lookout for which oils may be best suited for their purpose.

The visualizations below show the triglyceride properties of 6 common soapmaking oils, followed by the aggregate triglyceride properties of several soap base recipes collected from various published sources.

The hope is that these visualizations will provide an insight into good choices for oil compositions in soap base recipe design.

Coconut Oil

Coconut Oil is one of the most common raw material used in the soap and toiletry industry. It comes from the seeds of Cocos Nucifera and is primarily cultivated from Southeast Asia and the Philippines[3]

Jojoba Oil

Jojoba Oil is actually a liquid wax, enowned for its absorption and moisturizing abilities. Owing to its fatty acid make up, jojoba oil is very resistant to oxidation.[3]

Olive Oil

Olive oil gives a rich feel and helps to create a very hard and moisturizing bar once fully cured[3].

Shea Butter

Shea butter will start to melt on contact with the skin, making it an excellent choice for lip balms and lotion bars[3].

Hemp Oil

Hempseed Oil is an excellent source of important fatty acids such as Omega-6 Linoleic acid and Omega-3 Linoleic acid. In addition, it contains Gamma-Linoleic Acid (GLA) which helps to provide antioxidant protection[3].

Palm Kernel Oil

Palm oil and palm kernel oil might sound a lot alike, but they are quite different when it comes to their function in cosmetic formulations and soap making. Though both oils are derived from the palm fruit, palm oil comes from the mesocarp, or the fleshy part of the fruit, while palm kernel oil comes from the kernel at the fruitâ€™s core[4]
